JOURNAL

Kelly’s social ecosystem a mini experiment  

      Day 1: Sunday

Trying out my own idea is probably an important first step! Rather than trying to build something completely new at this early stage (there will be plenty of time for that later) I have decided to go with the tried and tested approach. I want to re-introduce something that I know I enjoy... but have left dormant for a while.

I took a moment to think about what I have enjoyed in the past about my activities and environments. Gardening came straight to mind. I absolutely LOVE having a garden, but until last month was living with no outside space. I could have made opportunities to garden but I let myself get snagged at the first hurdle. I have also been swept away returning to busy London life recently. Doing some gardening would be the perfect way to remind me of the importance of nurture, patience and grounding myself (quite literally). I am excited to get started!

Lesson of the day: When I didn't have a garden I could have helped a friend with gardening or asked for a patch in their space, or had a few plants indoors, or looked into getting an allotment. But I didn't. I love gardening, so what stopped me? It was the effort of that extra step needed. I hadn't really stopped to think about whether that small amount of extra effort would pay off because of just how much I enjoy it. Part of my ecosytem creation work will be to challenge the “I would like this, but I can’t have it right now” mentality. To think outside the box and consider whether making a bit of extra effort is worth it, even if what we end up with might be slightly different from the initial thought! Oh and just to add, that planning my “garden” right now is not what I would have normally considered as a space to garden in. It will consist of a few pots in my kitchen, and a small flower bed at the front of my house facing on to the road. But I am still excited to start!

Day 2: Monday 

It is bank holiday Monday, the sun is shining, the birds are singing and I am still half asleep - the perrrrrfect day for the garden centre. Perusing the choices of plants was so exciting and enticing, would I go for colourful flowers? Or scent? Or shrubs? I decided to opt for something new and try my hand at growing veggies (and some sweet peas of course, I can’t resist those). Starting to pick out the veggies and think of where I would put them (both inside and outside the house) I felt like my creative side was in charge!

I also remembered promising a friend that I would help her with her garden when she moved house last year. I am seeing her later today and thinking she could do with a pick me up I decided to add a few more plants and veggies to the trolley and surprise her with them!

Arriving at her house with an armful of vegetable plants made her and myself very happy! It was such a lovely afternoon pulling out the weeds, digging and watering the soil and planting the veggies. The actual activity was such a great metaphor for creating a social eco-system ...planning what the plants in the garden need, where to put them, making sure the environment is right, they have enough space and nutrients and the ongoing nurture thereafter, seeing how they do and making changes if needed.  It was also a wonderful way to spend time with a friend - we could catch up and chat whilst doing something that enriched both of us...and will hopefully yield a harvest!

Lesson of the day: We all have busy diaries, but social interaction is very important in our ecosystem… combining seeing a friend with an activity that I wanted to do as part of creating my own eco-system was a great idea! Not only because the activity can bear mutually beneficial...but it also adds motivation to make sure I did it!

My friends garden before and after

Day 3: Tuesday 

I had managed to get home in time to plant my own garden, as well as an indoor tomato plant and herb garden yesterday. On Tuesday morning I woke up 10 minutes earlier to water the seedlings. Normally I hit snooze so many times before dragging myself out of bed but today I was excited to get up and enjoy the creation!

When I went outside to water the veggies, to my horror I found that a cat had used my new patch as a litter box! I did not want this to become a norm and so took a few minutes to google how to protect my veggie patch!

In the evening I made a little mixture of spices from the kitchen and sprinkled it around the veggie patch hoping to encourage the cat to find a different litter tray!!

Lesson of the day : This experience made me think metaphorically. When creating your own ecosystem it is important to recognise and preserve boundaries to protect what you are creating. It might be that you share the idea with someone and they literally crap on the idea...or it might be that you create an environment open to others. This is great if you want them there, and it serves both of you. But occasionally they may use you or your ecosystem to ill effect, and that is when boundary setting and looking after your own interests is important!

Day 4: Wednesday 

It worked...no kitty litter space this morning but I am going to have to keep up the soil scenting (the neighbours must be questioning my sanity by now watching me grind pepper over the flower beds)!

Today I watered the patch in the evening too. It is street facing and an old man walked past and stopped for a few seconds. He was looking at the vegetable patch I had planted. He shook his head and said “this is lovely, I am so happy to see this”. We chatted for almost 20 minutes. He had lived round corner for about 15 years now and he said he had never seen anyone use the front garden. He was retired but used to be a chef, he didn't have family around and missed gardening himself and growing food. I told him to help himself to things as they grew as there would hopefully be enough to share. On walking away he said that the last 20 minutes had made his day. I felt so touched and in a way humbled that just a small veggie patch could make someone walking by so happy!

Lesson of the day: Social eco-systems have a two way flow! Our world around us affects us but we also affect our world. Taking care of yourself through creating your individual ecosytem is so important, it can have a huge impact on your quality of life and wellbeing. What is easy to forget is that it also has a ripple effect, to those we have relationships with day to day, but then also the community around us. Stopping and think about this, it really is quite a powerful thing!

Day 4: Thursday

I felt a sense of quiet satisfaction this morning as the tomato plant in the living room has grown quite a lot since I planted it just four days ago!! The one outside wasn't growing as fast, but still seemed to be doing ok. I was also feeling the effects of waking up a little earlier to water the plants everyday. It was nurturing and brought more mindfulness into the start of the day...which carried through the rest of the day. I hadn't quite realised how much value and peace a small routine involving nurture would add to my day,

Lesson of the day: I have a nurturing side that I don't often engage, because I have not made the opportunity to! In creating a personal ecosytem it is important to think about what parts of yourself you need to let out, or give space to that aren't getting the space they need. For me it was nurture, and I am sure there are a few more aspects I am yet to discover.

Day 5: Friday 

Today I went on holiday for a week and left my plants alone! I felt genuinely apprehensive at what would happen to them whilst i was away. I made sure they had lots of water this morning and crafted some slow drip water feeders using old bottles….fingers crossed they keep things alive! I realised that I didn't have neighbours I knew well enough to ask to water the plants. Last time I had a garden, if I went away (or even if I forgot) my next door neighbour would water my plants unprompted! What a lovely community feel that was.

Lesson of the day: I don't know my neighbours very well! This is fairly common place in London, I know. But I did love living on a street a few years ago where neighbours would talk and look after each other. Perhaps it is time to introduce myself as another step in creating the social ecosystem I want around me.
